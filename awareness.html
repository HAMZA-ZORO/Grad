About Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks.

Why Cybersecurity Matters

In our increasingly digital world, cybersecurity has become crucial for several reasons:

  • The average cost of a data breach is over $4 million
  • Cyber attacks occur every 39 seconds on average
  • Personal information can be sold on the dark web within seconds
  • 60% of small businesses close within 6 months of a cyber attack

Key Areas of Cybersecurity

  • Network Security: Protecting network infrastructure from unauthorized access
  • Application Security: Keeping software and devices free of threats
  • Information Security: Protecting the integrity and privacy of data
  • Operational Security: Processes for handling and protecting data assets

Common Cyber Threats

Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains one of the most dangerous cyber threats today. Attackers use increasingly sophisticated methods to trick users:

  • Email Phishing: Fake emails claiming to be from legitimate companies
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted attacks using personal information
  • Whaling: Attacks specifically targeting high-level executives
  • Vishing: Voice phishing using phone calls

Malware Types

  • Ransomware: Encrypts your files and demands payment
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors your activities
  • Trojans: Malicious programs disguised as legitimate software
  • Worms: Self-replicating malware that spreads across networks

How to Protect Yourself

Password Security

  • Length: Use passwords with at least 12 characters
  • Complexity: Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Uniqueness: Never reuse passwords across accounts
  • Password Manager: Use a reliable password manager

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds extra layers of security beyond just passwords:

  • Something you know: Password or PIN
  • Something you have: Phone or security key
  • Something you are: Fingerprint or face recognition
